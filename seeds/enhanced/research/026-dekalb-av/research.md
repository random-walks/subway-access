# DeKalb Av (ID: 26)

**Borough**: Brooklyn  
**Routes**: B D N Q R  
**Line**: 4th Avenue/Brighton  
**Division**: BMT

## Research Findings

DeKalb Av station became ADA accessible during a major overhaul completed between 2004 and 2006. The $36 million renovation was designed by architect Lee Harris Pomeroy and included the installation of three elevators: one from the street to the mezzanine (on the southeast corner of DeKalb Avenue and Flatbush Avenue) and two from the mezzanine to the platforms.

The project also included restoration of the station's original tiles and mosaics, consistent with Pomeroy's approach to modernizing stations while preserving historic elements. The station is located in Downtown Brooklyn.

The upgrade year is estimated as 2005, the midpoint of the 2004-2006 construction period. The exact elevator activation date within this range is not precisely documented.
