# WTC Cortlandt (ID: 328)

**Borough**: Manhattan  
**Routes**: 1  
**Line**: Broadway - 7Av  
**Division**: IRT

## Research Findings

The original Cortlandt Street station on the 1 train was destroyed in the September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks on the World Trade Center. The station was completely rebuilt and reopened as WTC Cortlandt on September 8, 2018 -- nearly 17 years after its destruction.

The rebuilt station was designed from the ground up with full ADA compliance, a significant upgrade over the original 1918 station which had no accessibility features. The new station includes elevators to each platform, ADA-compliant entrances, track-intrusion detection systems, fire alarms, Help Points, CCTV, countdown clocks, and air conditioning.

The upgrade year is 2018, matching the reopening date of the rebuilt station.

STV Inc. served as the engineering firm for the reconstruction. Judlau Contracting served as the general contractor. The project was funded through a combination of federal disaster recovery funding and MTA capital funds. The station's interior features a marble mosaic by artist Ann Hamilton incorporating text from the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.
