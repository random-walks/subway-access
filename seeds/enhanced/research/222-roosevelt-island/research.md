# Roosevelt Island (ID: 222)

**Borough**: Manhattan  
**Routes**: F  
**Line**: 63rd St  
**Division**: IND

## Research Findings

Roosevelt Island station opened on October 29, 1989 as part of the 63rd Street Line extension. The station was built new as part of the 1982-1986 MTA Capital Program and was designed with full accessibility from the start, including elevators and accessible platforms. Although the ADA was not enacted until 1990, the 63rd Street Line stations were constructed with accessibility features incorporated into the original design. The station is located deep underground on Roosevelt Island in the East River, and is one of the deepest stations in the system.
