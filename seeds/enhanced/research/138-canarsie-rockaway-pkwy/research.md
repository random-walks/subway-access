# Canarsie-Rockaway Pkwy (ID: 138)

**Borough**: Brooklyn  
**Routes**: L  
**Line**: Canarsie  
**Division**: BMT

## Research Findings

Canarsie-Rockaway Pkwy is a ground-level terminal station that was one of only four wheelchair-accessible stations in the entire New York City Subway system before 1988, accessible by virtue of its street-level design (not through an elevator retrofit). The other three pre-1988 accessible stations were Middle Village-Metropolitan Avenue, Rockaway Park-Beach 116th Street, and World Trade Center.

While the station was nominally accessible from the start, it was still missing some key ADA elements. As part of the 2015-2019 MTA Capital Program, the station received significant accessibility enhancements. In 2020, the MTA completed a three-part, year-long project ($5.48 million) that included: relocated station agent booths, extended and retrofitted platform boarding areas with warning strips and rubbing boards, reduced platform gaps, a new ADA-compliant ramp, new lighting and signage, and an upgraded PA system. The third phase was completed during the COVID-19 pandemic.

The upgrade year of 1987 is used as an approximate date for when the station was first considered wheelchair-accessible, predating the ADA itself.

## Notes

High confidence on pre-1988 accessibility due to ground-level design. The 2020 enhancements brought the station into fuller ADA compliance but the station was functionally accessible much earlier. Using 1987 as the upgrade year (pre-ADA ground-level access).
