# 86 St (ID: 476)

**Borough**: Manhattan  
**Routes**: Q  
**Line**: Second Av  
**Division**: IND

## Research Findings

The 86 St station opened on January 1, 2017 as part of the Second Avenue Subway Phase 1. This phase extended the Q train northward from 63 St-Lexington Av to 96 St along Second Avenue on the Upper East Side, creating three brand-new stations: 72 St, 86 St, and 96 St.

86 St is the middle station of the Phase 1 segment. Because it was built entirely new, it was designed with full ADA accessibility from the start, including elevators, escalators, and modern ADA-compliant entrances and platforms. The station also features commissioned artwork as part of the MTA Arts & Design program.

The upgrade year is 2017, matching the station's opening date.

The total Phase 1 project cost was approximately $4.45 billion, covering all three stations, tunneling, and associated infrastructure. This was funded through the 2010-2014 and 2015-2019 MTA Capital Programs with additional federal funding.
