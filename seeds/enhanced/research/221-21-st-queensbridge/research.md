# 21 St-Queensbridge (ID: 221)

**Borough**: Queens  
**Routes**: F  
**Line**: 63rd St  
**Division**: IND

## Research Findings

21 St-Queensbridge opened on October 29, 1989 as part of the 63rd Street Line extension. The station was built new as part of the 1982-1986 MTA Capital Program and was designed with full accessibility from the start, including elevators and accessible platforms. Although the ADA was not enacted until 1990, the 63rd Street Line stations were constructed with accessibility features incorporated into the original design. The line was famously known as the "tunnel to nowhere" from 1989 to 2001, as it initially only connected to the BMT Broadway Line and did not reach Queens Boulevard until the 63rd Street connector opened in December 2001.
