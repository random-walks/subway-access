# South Ferry (ID: 330)

**Borough**: Manhattan  
**Routes**: 1  
**Line**: Broadway - 7Av  
**Division**: IRT

## Research Findings

The new South Ferry station opened on March 16, 2009, replacing the original 1905 loop station. The old station had a tight loop configuration that could only accommodate the first five cars of a ten-car train, requiring passengers to walk forward before exiting. The new station was built as a conventional straight platform that serves all cars.

Because the 2009 station was entirely new construction, it was built with full ADA accessibility from the start, including 2 elevators, 7 escalators, and ADA-compliant entrances and platforms. The original construction cost was approximately $530 million, funded primarily through federal 9/11 recovery funds (the project was part of Lower Manhattan's post-9/11 infrastructure rebuilding).

The station was severely damaged by Hurricane Sandy on October 29, 2012, when the tunnel and station were flooded with seawater. The MTA temporarily reopened the old loop station while the new station underwent extensive repairs. The rebuilt new station reopened on June 27, 2017.

For accessibility tracking purposes, the upgrade year is 2009 -- when the station first became ADA-accessible. The 2017 reopening after Sandy repairs restored that existing accessibility rather than creating it.
