# 34 St-Hudson Yards (ID: 471)

**Borough**: Manhattan  
**Routes**: 7  
**Line**: Flushing  
**Division**: IRT

## Research Findings

The 34 St-Hudson Yards station opened on September 13, 2015 as part of the 7 Line Extension, the first major expansion of the New York City subway system since the 1980s. The extension brought the 7 train westward from Times Square to the Hudson Yards development on Manhattan's far west side.

Because this was an entirely new-construction station, it was built with full ADA accessibility from the start. The station includes elevators, escalators, and modern ADA-compliant features throughout. There is no prior inaccessible version of this station -- it did not exist before 2015.

The upgrade year is 2015, matching the station's opening date.

The total project cost for the 7 Line Extension was approximately $2.4 billion, covering the tunnel boring, station construction, and all related infrastructure. A station-specific cost breakdown is not publicly available. The project was funded through the 2005-2009 and 2010-2014 MTA Capital Programs, with significant financing from the City of New York through the Hudson Yards Infrastructure Corporation.
